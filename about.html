About Me

UX Architect exploring how AI can enhance human experiences while upholding dignity, equity, and inclusion.

The Story So Far...

From Developer to Designer

My path into UX started with code. As a developer, I built interfaces and systems, but I became increasingly fascinated by the people using them. I found myself asking: What makes this intuitive? Why does this confuse users? How can we make this more inclusive?

That curiosity led me to transition from pure development into UX design. I brought my technical foundation with me—understanding constraints, feasibility, and implementation gave me a unique lens for solving design problems. But I also learned to lead with empathy, research, and human needs rather than technical capabilities.

Evolving as a UX Generalist

As a UX generalist, I immersed myself in the full spectrum of user experience work: research, interaction design, information architecture, usability testing, and design systems. I learned to balance user needs with business goals, advocate for accessibility, and collaborate across multidisciplinary teams.

This phase taught me that great UX isn't about any single skill—it's about synthesizing insights from research, translating them into clear design decisions, and ensuring those decisions serve diverse users equitably. I became passionate about inclusive design and began to understand how our design choices can either open doors or create barriers.

Growing into UX Architecture

Now as a UX Architect, I work at a more strategic level—shaping systems, establishing patterns, and ensuring coherence across complex products and services. But I'm also at an inflection point: AI is transforming how we design, research, and create experiences.

That's why I'm investing time in learning about AI—not just the technical mechanics, but its implications for users, communities, and society. I'm exploring how AI can enhance research methods, accelerate insight generation, enable rapid prototyping, and support better decision-making. But I'm equally focused on the ethical dimensions: bias, fairness, transparency, consent, and dignity.

My focus areas include conversational design (how we design natural interactions with AI), AI-assisted research (using AI to augment, not replace, human insight), inclusive AI (ensuring AI serves all communities equitably), and ethical AI practices (building systems that respect human rights and dignity).

What Drives My Work

Diversity & Inclusion

Technology should work for everyone, not just the people who build it. I'm committed to inclusive design practices that center the experiences of marginalized communities, challenge assumptions, and ensure that AI systems don't perpetuate historical inequities. This means actively seeking diverse perspectives, questioning default choices, and advocating for accessibility as a baseline—not an afterthought.

Social Justice

Design is political. Every choice we make about features, data, algorithms, and interfaces has implications for power, equity, and justice. I approach UX work with an understanding that we're not just building products—we're shaping how people interact with information, services, and each other. That responsibility requires us to ask hard questions about who benefits, who might be harmed, and how we can do better.

Ethical AI

AI has tremendous potential to enhance human capabilities, but it also carries risks—algorithmic bias, privacy violations, manipulative design, and loss of agency. I believe in building AI systems that are transparent, accountable, and aligned with human values. This means prioritizing consent, explainability, fairness, and the ability for people to understand and contest automated decisions that affect their lives.

Learning in Public

I'm documenting my AI learning journey openly because I believe knowledge grows through sharing. By making my process visible—including mistakes, uncertainties, and evolving understanding—I hope to demystify AI for other UX practitioners and create space for honest conversations about what we're all figuring out together.
