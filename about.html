About Me

Bicultural. Empathic. Knowledgeable.

As a UX consultant of mixed Māori and Pākehā heritage, I bring a bicultural worldview to every project. At Signify Ltd, I focus on government digital services that truly serve all people using ethical frameworks to achieve inclusion and equity.

My practice is rooted in the belief that technology should honor diverse perspectives and empower communities. I approach design as a systems thinker, understanding the interconnected nature of digital experiences.

"Mā te kimi ka kite, Mā te kite ka mōhio, Mā te mōhio ka mārama"

Seek and discover. Discover and know. Know and become enlightened.

Core Disciplines

Five interconnected areas of expertise that inform my holistic approach to digital design.

UX Research

Deep understanding of human behavior through qualitative and quantitative methods. I uncover genuine user needs, not assumptions, using ethnographic research, usability testing, and psychology-informed analysis.

Key Capabilities

  • User interviews and contextual inquiry
  • Usability testing and heuristic evaluation
  • Survey design and statistical analysis
  • Journey mapping and persona development
  • Behavioral psychology and cognitive science application

Approach

I prioritize user advocacy at every stage, ensuring voices from marginalized communities are heard and centered. Research isn't just data collection—it's about understanding the full human experience.

Information Architecture

Creating clear, intuitive structures that help people find what they need. I design taxonomies, navigation systems, and content hierarchies that make complex information accessible.

Key Capabilities

  • Content audits and inventory management
  • Taxonomy and metadata design
  • Card sorting and tree testing
  • Navigation design and wayfinding
  • Search strategy and findability optimization

Approach

Good IA is invisible—it just works. I focus on mental models and user expectations, creating structures that feel natural and reduce cognitive load, especially in government services where clarity is critical.

Accessibility

Well versed in WCAG 2.2 and 3.0, ensuring digital experiences work for everyone. Accessibility isn't a checkbox—it's about creating inclusive experiences from the ground up.

Key Capabilities

  • WCAG 2.2 AA and AAA compliance testing
  • WCAG 3.0 preparation and implementation
  • Screen reader optimization (NVDA, JAWS, VoiceOver)
  • Keyboard navigation and focus management
  • ARIA implementation and semantic HTML
  • Color contrast and visual design accessibility

Approach

Accessibility is a human right, not a feature. I advocate for inclusive design practices that consider cognitive, motor, visual, and auditory disabilities. Every design decision considers the full spectrum of human ability.

Ethical AI

Building AI systems that respect human dignity, reduce bias, and promote equity. I apply indigenous frameworks and constitutional AI principles to create responsible technology.

Key Capabilities

  • AI bias detection and mitigation
  • Fairness testing and algorithmic auditing
  • Privacy-preserving design principles
  • Transparency and explainability frameworks
  • Governance structures for AI implementation

Approach

I believe indigenous perspectives—particularly concepts like whakapapa (interconnectedness) and kaitiakitanga (guardianship)—offer powerful alternatives to Western hierarchical models of AI. My work centers community benefit and long-term impact.

Conversation Design

Crafting natural, inclusive dialogue for chatbots, voice interfaces, and AI systems. I design conversations that feel human, respect cultural context, and serve diverse users.

Key Capabilities

  • Conversational flow design and mapping
  • Intent modeling and entity recognition
  • Persona development for AI agents
  • Error handling and recovery strategies
  • Multilingual and culturally adaptive design

Approach

Effective conversation design requires deep empathy and cultural awareness. I design for flexibility and context, ensuring AI interactions feel respectful and appropriate across diverse user groups.

Guiding Principles

Equity First

Technology should redistribute power, not reinforce existing hierarchies. Every project begins with the question: who benefits, and who is left out?

Bicultural Wisdom

Drawing on Māori concepts of whanaungatanga (relationships) and manaakitanga (care for others) alongside Western methodologies.

Systems Thinking

Understanding interconnections, power structures, and long-term impacts. Design decisions ripple through communities—we must design responsibly.

Transparent Process

Accessible communication over corporate jargon. Direct collaboration over hierarchical structures. Warmth and authenticity in every interaction.
