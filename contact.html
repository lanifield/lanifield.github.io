Frequently Asked Questions

What services do you offer?

I specialize in UX research, information architecture, conversation design, ethical AI implementation, and accessibility consulting (WCAG 2.2 & 3.0). I also offer workshops and training on these topics.

What types of projects are you most interested in?

I'm particularly drawn to projects that center equity, inclusion, and ethical technology. Government digital services, AI governance, and initiatives that redistribute power rather than reinforcing existing hierarchies.

Do you work with international clients?

Yes! While I'm based in Wellington, Aotearoa New Zealand, I work with clients globally. I'm experienced in remote collaboration and can work across time zones.

What's your approach to accessibility?

Accessibility isn't a checkbox—it's about creating inclusive experiences from the ground up. I follow WCAG 2.2 AA standards as a baseline and advocate for exceeding compliance to truly serve all users.

How do you approach ethical AI?

I blend indigenous frameworks (particularly Māori concepts of interconnectedness and guardianship) with constitutional AI principles. My focus is on bias mitigation, transparency, and ensuring AI systems benefit communities rather than extracting from them.

What's your typical project timeline?

Project timelines vary based on scope, but I typically work on engagements ranging from 2-week sprints to multi-month partnerships. I'll provide a detailed timeline during our scoping discussions.
